Get the Right Amount

When it comes to feeding your dog, it's all about finding that sweet spot. Feed them too little, and they'll be hangry (yes, that's a thing); feed them too much, and they'll be, well, a little too round. But don't worry, it's not rocket science (although, if your dog could do rocket science, that would be pretty cool). A good rule of thumb is to check the dog food serving size chart, which usually comes with the food or can be found on the manufacturer's website. This will give you a rough idea of how much to feed your dog based on their age, weight, and activity level. Just remember, every dog is different, so be prepared to make some adjustments as you go.

Vet-Approved Feeding

So, how much food should you actually be giving your dog? According to the experts (aka vets), it's all about balance. You want to make sure your furry friend is getting the nutrients they need to stay healthy and happy, without overdoing it. A general rule of thumb is to feed your dog between 2-3% of their body weight per day, divided into 2-3 meals. Of course, this can vary depending on your dog's individual needs, but it's a good starting point. And remember, it's always better to err on the side of caution тАУ if you're unsure, it's always best to consult with your vet, who can give you personalized advice tailored to your dog's specific needs.
