Specialized Tools for Deep Research and Synthesis

Beyond general-purpose large language models, there is a whole world of specialized AI designed specifically for the rigorous demands of researchers. Platforms like Perplexity and Elicit have become my go-to sources when I need to find peer-reviewed evidence to back up a specific claim. These tools don't just generate text; they provide direct links to the DOI and original source, which is critical for maintaining academic integrity. I've found that using ChatGPT for initial brainstorming and then switching to Perplexity for fact-checking creates a workflow that is both creative and academically sound. The evolution of these tools in 2025 and 2026 has been nothing short of spectacular, with better context windows and a more sophisticated understanding of academic jargon.
